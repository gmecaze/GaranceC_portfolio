Embassador

emlyon Business School


As an embassador, my role is to represent and promote emlyon business school programs and services to future students by sharing my personal experience. Online, during fairs, or campus days, we answer to all questions related to the school or the program.

Being an embassador is not only a role, it is a way of being: outgoing, we enjoy meeting new people. Talking with strangers is not a problem and we do everything to make them feel comfortable.

Emlyon embassador are easy to recognize during fairs: with their amazing red varsity jacket, they are always smiling, laughing and even sometimes: dancing!

Student Council Associate

Solbridge - South Korea


After two weeks of recruitment, I was chosen by the Student Council to become an associate. This role consists in ensuring logistic, organization and communication of the various events of the school.

During my semester at SolBridge, we organized no less than five cultural trips across South Korea, and more than a dozen events for the school. From sporting events to artistic Talent Show, our Council was very proactive and it was extremely fun to be part of the adventure.

One of my fondest memories of this semester is to have been chosen Master of Ceremony for the Graduation party. With a twist of ‘Breakfast at Tiffany’s’, we welcomed around 300 guests for an unforgettable night.

Volunteer

Geriatric Hospital


Thanks to my high school, I had the chance to participate in this touching inter-generational exchange project. The principle is simple: every week at lunchtime, student volunteers go to the geriatric hospital of Fourvière to spend a special time with an elderly person.

Thanks to this project, I met an incredible person, whom I had the pleasure of meeting every week for a walk in the park, a birthday cake, a piano session, and many other special moments. This lonely person had no other visits, and coming so often with other students, we realized the distress and loneliness of some of our elders.

This great experience taught me to take more time for my own grandparents and for seniors in general.
